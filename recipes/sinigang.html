Sinigang

Description

Sinigang is a Filipino sour soup or stew characterized by its distinct sour flavor. It is commonly made with pork, beef, shrimp, or fish, and a variety of vegetables such as water spinach, daikon radish, and eggplant. The sourness of the soup comes from tamarind, calamansi, or green mango. Sinigang is a popular comfort food in the Philippines and is often served with steamed rice.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound pork ribs
  • 1 onion, quartered
  • 2 tomatoes, quartered
  • 1 radish, sliced
  • 1 eggplant, sliced

Steps

  1. In a large pot, combine pork ribs, onion, tomatoes, and enough water to cover the ingredients. Bring to a boil and simmer until the pork is tender.
  2. Add radish and eggplant to the pot and cook until the vegetables are tender.
  3. Season the soup with tamarind paste, fish sauce, and salt to taste. Serve hot with steamed rice.
